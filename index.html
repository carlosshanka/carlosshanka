About

Left: COP27, Sharm el-Sheikh, 2022  ·  Right: Field production, Outlaw Ocean Project

"The most urgent call to act doesn't come from charts or models. It comes from a person, in a place, telling you what they can no longer afford to lose — and it demands you do something about it."

Carlos Shanka Boissy Diaz is a Canarian-Senegalese climate researcher and documentary filmmaker working at the intersection of political ecology, water governance, and Atlantic island futures. He holds an MA in Development Studies from the Institute of Development Studies (IDS) at the University of Sussex, and a BSc in Biology from the University of Bristol.

He is a UNESCO Man and the Biosphere (MAB) Young Scientist Awardee, conducting research on water governance in Lanzarote, and a fellow of The Outlaw Ocean Project, where he carried out investigative field research in Senegal and the Canary Islands. He is the founding president of Global Shapers Las Palmas, a World Economic Forum initiative for young leaders.

His documentary work examines migration, resource extraction, and the politics of scarcity in island and coastal communities. He approaches storytelling as a form of governance research — grounded in community, attentive to power, and committed to bringing findings back to the places from which they are drawn.

He works in Spanish, English, French, and Portuguese.
